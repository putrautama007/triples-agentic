# UX Research

## Core Principle
Design decisions backed by evidence, not assumptions. Research de-risks product bets early and cheaply.

## Research Methods by Maturity

### Discovery (What problem to solve)
- **User interviews** — 1:1, 45–60 min, semi-structured; uncover mental models, pain points, and jobs-to-be-done
- **Contextual inquiry** — observe users in their natural environment; reveals what people do vs. what they say
- **Diary studies** — longitudinal; captures behavior over time, not just in a lab session

### Validation (Does the solution work)
- **Usability testing** — 5 users catch ~85% of usability issues (Nielsen); moderated or unmoderated
- **Concept testing** — evaluate early prototypes or storyboards before building
- **A/B testing** — compare two variants with live traffic; requires sufficient volume

### Measurement (How well is it working)
- **Surveys (SUS, SUPR-Q)** — quantify satisfaction and usability at scale
- **Analytics review** — funnel drop-off, task completion rates, error rates
- **NPS / CSAT** — track longitudinal perception changes

## Interview Best Practices
- Ask about past behavior, not hypothetical future behavior: "Tell me about the last time you did X" not "Would you use X?"
- Use the 5 Whys to reach root causes
- Never lead: "What was that experience like?" not "Was it frustrating?"
- One question at a time; silence is not a problem — let users think

## Usability Test Protocol
1. **Screener** — recruit 5–8 participants matching primary persona
2. **Task list** — 3–5 realistic scenario-based tasks, no UI hints
3. **Think-aloud** — ask participants to narrate as they go
4. **Debrief** — post-task questions: "What did you expect to happen?", "What was confusing?"
5. **Synthesis** — affinity mapping; group observations into themes; note severity (critical/serious/minor)

## Synthesis Output
- **Affinity map** — clustered raw observations → themes
- **Insight statement** — `[User type] struggles with [behavior] because [root cause]`
- **Opportunity statement** — `How might we [enable user to do X] so that [outcome]?`
